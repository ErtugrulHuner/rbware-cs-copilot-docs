# Maintenance

_Source: `manual_maintenance.md` (Rainbow Robotics RB Cobot Docs)_

**Warning:** cut power to the system (control box and robot arm) before any maintenance. For pneumatic/electric line-passing models, disconnect the connected energy source first.

## Check list and period
| Item | Check point | Period |
|---|---|---|
| Robot Arm | Moves to desired location properly | Daily |
| Robot Arm | Keeps pose between on/off; remove stains/dust/contamination | Every 3 months |
| Motor | Irregular heat or noise | Daily |
| Screws | All body screws tightened | Every 3 months |
| Control Box | Cable connections | Every 6 months |
| Control Box | Remove dust inside | Every 6 months (see below) |

If an issue during inspection can't be resolved, contact the manufacturer.

## Control box maintenance
Dust buildup can cause overheating or static damage. Inspect/clean at least every 6 months (more often in dustier environments).
1. Turn off the control box.
2. Remove the cover.
3. Check for accumulated dust.
4. Remove dust carefully with a vacuum cleaner or similar tool.
5. Check all wires are connected properly.

## Robot arm maintenance
Inspect at least once a year (more often depending on wear).
1. Move the robot to the **Home** position.
2. Turn off the control box.
3. Check: robot–control box cable (cut/stabbed?), screws (loose?), mechanical parts — motor/brake/reduction gear (louder than normal?).
4. Remove stains, dust, and contamination.

See [Field Confirmed Corrections and Precedents](/policy/field-confirmed-corrections.md) for hardware failure diagnostics beyond routine maintenance (solenoid switch failure, brake looseness, comm errors, control-box faults) and when to route to 출장AS/입고 instead of self-service.
